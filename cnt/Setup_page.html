Rayleigh number is a dimensionless number associated with buoyancy-driven flow, also known as natural convection.

It characterises the fluid's flow regime: below a critical value, denotes laminar flow, and heat transfer is by conduction rather than convection. Over the critical value, it denotes turbulent flow with convection cells.



Prandtl number is defined as the ratio of momentum diffusivity to thermal diffusivity:

For Pr ≪ 1 the thermal diffusivity dominatest the behavior, while for Pr ≫ 1 the momentum diffusivity dominates.



Reynolds number helps predict flow patterns in different fluid flow situations.

At low Reynolds numbers, flows tends to be laminar, while at high Reynolds numbers flows tend to be turbulent.
